01 · Taste, craft, point of view

Three different things

Silicon Valley loves the word "taste," but Dylan pulls apart three overlapping ideas. Taste is navigating the possibilities and having clear preferences you can articulate. Craft is pushing past where others stop — making every level of abstraction, micro to macro, fit together. Point of view is expressing something unique that moves a conversation forward.

If everyone agrees to your point of view, you're probably not having much of a point of view.— Dylan Field

Listen to users to reach local maxima, he says — but point of view is what gets you to the global one.

02 · Can AI learn taste?

The possibility space

Modern models (he points to Gemini 3.x) can produce incredible visual output with the right prompt and references. But that's the point — they "light up the possibility space," they don't hand you the answer.

03 · Idea → product

It's not a line anymore

The old linear pipeline — ideation → alignment → design → production — is gone. Now you hop around: start anywhere, go everywhere, in stacked diamonds of divergence and convergence.

04 · Code ↔ canvas

Why the round trip matters

On why Figma added code→canvas (not just canvas→code): for adjusting spacing, color, and layout, direct manipulation beats everything.

05 · You're the judge

A prompt is clay, not an answer

On the wave of prompt-to-design tools and "swarm of agents" demos: impressive, but the output of a prompt is not a final result.

06 · Everyone's a builder

PMs get to make things

A striking stat: ~60% of designs in Figma are now created by non-designers. Research, support, and sales ship functioning prototypes, not just reports — and PMs get to build, not just write decks for upward review.

07 · Talk to customers

Don't be the bottleneck

Despite all the internal tooling, his push is outward: always be talking to customers, and don't let one reviewer gate everything (the "20 rounds before Dylan sees it" trap).

08 · Design is the new code

Toward a renaissance

Where it's heading: designing visual-first and issuing a pull request to production straight from Figma — value moving up the stack from the detailed spec (code) to the blueprint.
