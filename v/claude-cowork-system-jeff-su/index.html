02 · The whole system

Two plain-text files

Behind the magic: a parent folder ("Claude OS") with two markdown files. claude.md is the instruction manual telling Cowork how to behave; it points to other files (memory, voice principles, governance) only when needed.

These are just simple text files, so don't be intimidated… claude.md is the instruction manual that tells Cowork how to behave, and it points to other files whenever it needs more detail. That's it. That's the entire system.— Jeff Su

03 · The mental model

A Constitution, then state laws

The analogy that makes it click: the root claude.md is the US Constitution — it applies everywhere. Each workstation is a state law that stacks on top. So working on your newsletter, Cowork obeys the root rules plus the newsletter-specific ones.

04 · Persistent memory

Two lines do the work

Cowork remembers across sessions because of two rules in claude.md: read memory.md at the start of every session, and when I say "remember this," write it to memory.md. The file holds your active projects and a running memory log.

05 · The real point

Surface your implied context

The deeper idea: most bad AI output comes from missing implied context — the stuff you know but forget to state every time (your writing style, your active projects, how you like email structured). The files exist to hold that for you.

A voice-principles.md file bootstraps this by extracting your patterns from your last ~30 sent emails (or five writing samples), so drafts sound like you.

06 · Workstations

One folder per area of your life

Below the root sit workstations, each with its own claude.md/memory.md/resources. Universal ones (Email HQ) apply across everything; dedicated ones (Personal Finances) own one area. Feed the finances workstation a year of statements and it builds a categorized spending tracker.

How to startAfter ~30 workstations, his advice is to start slow: build two or three, get a feel for how claude.md and memory.md interact, and only add more when a real need shows up.

07 · Keep it lean

Save tokens and rate limits

Two pro tips. A /session-audit skill scans the conversation at the end for unsaved preferences to remember. And to avoid burning your limits:

  1. Root claude.md under 300 linesit loads every session; point to resource files instead of inlining detail.
  2. Don't repeat rulesa rule in voice-principles shouldn't be restated in Email HQ.
  3. Default to Sonnet~80% of tasks don't need Opus, and Sonnet is ~1/5 the cost — switch to Opus only for 3+ interdependent steps.
The rules, the memory, the patterns compound every single session, and you will always be ahead of someone who starts even a day after you.— Jeff Su
