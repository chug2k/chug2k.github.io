I. The Verdict

The Tribunal finds, on the plain language of the Pact, that Outcome B has occurred.

Party C (C.L.) achieved full unemployment by his deadline of May 15, 2026. Party K (K.J.) did not achieve unemployment by June 5, 2026 — his own birthday, originally selected for symbolism and hereby repurposed as a monument to cowardice.

Under Clause 2(B), the consequence is unambiguous: Party K must fly to Vietnam, at his own expense, to witness the good life he is denying himself.

Tribunal's note: Exhibit A is dispositive. In four messages Party K (a) confesses the promotion and equity grant, (b) pegs his exit to the vesting date rather than his deadline, and (c) reframes the punishment flight as a one-way move. Each admission is treated below.

The Tribunal assigns the defense exactly zero weight, for the following reasons:

A relocation is not a punishment trip. The obligation under the Pact was a flight, undertaken in June 2026, of a minimum 3-day duration, for the express purpose of punishment and/or witnessing liberation. Party K's own words — “Oct 1st week I'll be in VN” — describe a one-way move four months late. It satisfies none of the required elements: wrong month, wrong nature, wrong intent.

The No-Weaseling Clause governs. Clause 5 expressly bars reliance on technicalities, creative reinterpretation, and the conversion of a present obligation into a vague future intention. Exhibit A shows Party K timing his entire exit around a stock vesting date (“vest on Sept 15th” → “2 weeks notice on Sept 16th”) — the precise species of weaseling the clause was drafted to extinguish. The Tribunal further notes that one does not buy a relocation timeline from a man this eager to collect his equity first.

Aggravating factor — the raise. The record — in Party K's own hand — establishes that his failure was not inertia or bad luck. He reneged because he was promoted and granted stock, an affirmative election of compensation over liberation, the very trap the Pact was designed to spring him from. This is an aggravating circumstance, not a mitigating one. It also forecloses any plea of poverty: by his own admission Party K is about to be materially richer than when he signed, and is amply able to satisfy the settlement below.

III. The Substitution — An Act of Grace

Party C, exercising mercy not required of him, offers a one-time substitution: Party K need not physically board the aircraft, provided he donates the full equivalent cost of the trip to a charity of Party C's choosing.

Party C has nominated SOS Children's Villages as beneficiary. The rationale is recorded in Section V below and is, frankly, better than Party K deserves.

This substitution is offered in lieu of, not in addition to, the flight obligation — and is conditioned strictly on the terms in Section VI.

IV. Assessment of Damages

“Full equivalent cost” is the true all-in cost of the trip Party K would otherwise take — not merely the airfare, but every line item a real visit incurs, door to door. The Tribunal declines the fiction of a 3-day trip: nobody flies 19+ hours each way to Vietnam for three days. The Pact's 3-day minimum is a floor, not a forecast. The honest trip — the one Party K would genuinely take — runs 5 to 6 days, priced at June 2026 rates (one of the two most expensive months of the year on this route; no off-season red-eye may be used to deflate it).

The true all-in cost is approximately $2,272. The Tribunal, in a gesture of goodwill it will likely regret, rounds the figure down to a clean $2,000 — a discount of nearly $300, extended purely to make Party K feel better about a situation that is entirely his own doing. He is not free to round any further down.

All figures are estimates aggregated over recent days and subject to change. The settlement figure is a fair valuation, not a guaranteed bookable price. Departure measured from LAX, the practical international gateway for Irvine; no direct LAX–SGN service exists, so all itineraries connect (~19+ hrs). The Tribunal has deliberately erred toward conservative, non-inflated numbers throughout.

VI. Terms of Settlement

  • The donation shall equal the assessed figure of $2,000 USD (the ~$2,272 all-in cost of a realistic trip, mercifully rounded down) or greater, paid to SOS Children's Villages.
  • Party K shall furnish proof of donation — a receipt or confirmation — to Party C. No receipt, no settlement.
  • Absent a valid receipt, the original Clause 2(B) flight obligation snaps back into full force, including the 3-day minimum stay and all travel at Party K's own expense.
  • This substitution is a one-time act of grace. Any attempt to weasel on the donation revives both the Flight Terms and the No-Weaseling Clause, to be applied with prejudice.
  • The October relocation, if and when it occurs, is noted with warmth and assigned no settlement value whatsoever.
So ordered.
